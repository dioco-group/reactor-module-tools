# ALC LLA Book 4 — image markup notes

Per-page guidance for the image-markup / image-generation steps. PDF page indices
(3-digit, matching `page_XXX_*` filenames), not printed page numbers.

- **All pages:** ignore page bleed-through / show-through (faint ghosted or mirrored
  marks from the reverse side of the sheet or the facing page). Never reproduce it;
  only the solid intended foreground line art is the illustration. (Page 007 in
  particular had bleed-through reproduced into `page_007_006`.)

- **All pages:** panel letters and caption words printed with the art (e.g. "B."
  and "football" beneath a panel) are book apparatus, NOT part of the illustration.
  Generate ONLY the line-art picture — no letter labels, no caption text.
  (Page 007's sports panels had "B. football" etc. reproduced into the images.)

- **Page 012 (Lesson 1A, Figure 8 — "Linda's week"):** the four story panels are a
  single framed strip but represent FOUR separate scenes. Mark them up as FOUR
  separate illustrations (one per panel), so each narration line can show its own
  panel — do NOT group them into one image.

- **Page 015 (Lesson 1B, Figure 6 — downtown San Antonio):** the illustration is a
  THREE-panel strip (downtown cityscape / man looking up at skyscrapers / couple at
  the "Le Gilles" restaurant window). Mark it up as THREE separate illustrations
  (one per panel), so each dialogue line can show its own scene — do NOT group
  them into one image.
